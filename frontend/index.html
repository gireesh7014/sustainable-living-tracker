Introduction

A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases (GHGs) emitted into the atmosphere as a result of human activities, typically expressed in equivalent tons of carbon dioxide (CO2e).

Human activities such as burning fossil fuels for energy, deforestation, and industrial processes release significant amounts of CO2 and other GHGs into the atmosphere. These emissions contribute to global warming and climate change, which have far-reaching impacts on the environment, human health, and economies around the world.

Reducing your carbon footprint involves making choices that minimize your contribution to these emissions. This can include using energy more efficiently, reducing waste, traveling sustainably, and supporting policies and initiatives aimed at reducing carbon emissions globally.
